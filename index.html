Where it all started

While working in different organizations, I noticed the same patterns that keep teams from being productive. While people were busy, releases were slow and error-prone and customers unhappy. The features that were rolled didn't solve the customer needs, resulting in bloated processes that inhibited rather than improved the customer journey.

Everyone was frustrated by the amount of rework to get while organizational debt kept increasing. Team members were demotivated, never given adequate time to deal with organizational debt and improve, restricted by a lack of ownerships and silo's competing with each other internally. There was clearly no winner. The outcome was a lot of wasted effort. I realized that productivity is not so much about speed or efficiency, but about accuracy and relationships.

The funficient way was born to help you optimize your most precious resource - time.

The four deadly time waste sins

What's your biggest time waster?

Productivity isn't just about being faster or doing more. Optimal productivity is using the minimum inputs to get the maximum usable outputs. It's not done until someone uses and loves it.

delivering unused features

Spending time going through an entire delivery cycle with no-one using it is 100% wasted effort.

post-production rework

The leaky bucket. It's always more expensive to redo something later.

overly complicated solutions

It's more costly in maintenance, and might cost you your users looking for a simpler solution.

unused skills

By far the biggest waste is not fully utilizing people's talents and skills.
