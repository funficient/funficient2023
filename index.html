Where it all started

While working in different organizations, I noticed the same patterns that keep teams from being more productive. While people were busy, releases were slow and error-prone. The features rolled out didn't meet the customer needs. Although there were many features added, they didn't come together in a coherent system, resulting in bloated workflows that inhibited rather than improved the customer journey.

Everyone was frustrated by the amount of rework required and organizational debt kept increasing. Team members were demotivated, restricted by a lack of ownerships and silo's competing with each other internally. There was clearly no winner. The outcome was a lot of wasted time and effort. I realized that productivity is not so much about speed or efficiency, but rather accuracy and relationships.

The funficient way was born to help you optimize your most precious resources. People and Time.

What's your biggest time waster?

Productivity isn't just about being faster or doing more. Optimal productivity is getting the maximum usable outputs with minimum inputs. It's not done until someone uses and loves it.

delivering unused features

Spending time going through an entire delivery cycle with no-one using it is 100% wasted effort.

post-production rework

The leaky bucket. It's always more expensive to redo something later.

overly complicated solutions

It's more costly in maintenance, and might cost you your users looking for a simpler solution.

unused skills

By far the biggest waste is not fully utilizing people's talents and skills.
